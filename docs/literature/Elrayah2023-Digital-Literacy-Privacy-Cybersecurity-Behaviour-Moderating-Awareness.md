- Elrayah and Jamil (2023), in a study of 235 individuals in Saudi Arabia, further demonstrated that digital literacy—including copyright awareness, responsible citizenship, content curation, and online connectedness—significantly predicted cybersecurity behaviour, with cybersecurity awareness serving as a positive moderator of these relationships.
- ---  ## 2.3 Empirical Review  ### 2.3.1 Relationship between Cybersecurity Awareness and Digital Behaviour  The relationship between cybersecurity awareness and digital behaviour is one of the most extensively studied questions in the information security literature, and the findings are remarkably consistent: awareness is a necessary but not sufficient condition for secure digital behaviour (Kovačević et al., 2020; Anwar et al., 2023; Elrayah & Jamil, 2023).
- Elrayah and Jamil (2023) examined this relationship among 235 individuals in Saudi Arabia, finding that digital literacy—an enabler and correlate of cybersecurity awareness—significantly predicted cybersecurity behaviour across all four of their digital literacy dimensions (copyright, citizenship, curation, and connectedness).
- Elrayah and Jamil (2023) found that cybersecurity awareness moderated the effect of digital literacy on cybersecurity behaviour, implying that awareness does not act solely as an independent predictor of risk mitigation but also shapes the pathways through which other factors (including digital behaviour practices) affect protective outcomes.
