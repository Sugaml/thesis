## Evidence Mapped to Research Title
_Research title: Assessing the Influence of Cybersecurity Awareness on Digital Behaviour and Risk Mitigation Practices Among University Students in Nepal_

- In the cybersecurity literature, digital behaviour is operationalised through specific security-relevant practices: password management, social media usage patterns, responses to suspicious online content, software update habits, and the extent to which users verify the authenticity of digital communications before acting on them (Mai & Tick, 2021; Jangid, 2025).
- Jangid (2025) found that among college students, 64% used the same password across multiple platforms and 58% had clicked on unknown or suspicious links, despite 90% having heard of phishing.
- Jangid (2025) found that while 70% of students had antivirus software installed, only 30% regularly updated their software and applications, suggesting partial adoption of risk mitigation practices.
- Jangid (2025) found that although 90% of college students had heard of phishing, only 35% could correctly identify a phishing email, and 58% had clicked on unknown or suspicious links.
- ### 2.3.3 Relationship between Digital Behaviour and Risk Mitigation  The relationship between digital behaviour and risk mitigation practices is bidirectional and mutually reinforcing, but the dominant causal direction in the literature runs from safer digital behaviour to stronger risk mitigation (Jangid, 2025; Alqahtani, 2022).
- Jangid (2025) observed that the minority of students who consistently practised safe digital behaviour (checking URLs, enabling 2FA, using separate passwords) also reported the highest adoption of risk mitigation tools, including regularly updated antivirus software and encrypted communication applications.
