## Evidence Mapped to Research Title
_Research title: Assessing the Influence of Cybersecurity Awareness on Digital Behaviour and Risk Mitigation Practices Among University Students in Nepal_

- Bhandari (2025) reported that among Nepalese university students, only 46.9% showed awareness of Nepal's cybersecurity legal framework, even though 67.2% were familiar with the concept of "hacking," highlighting a critical disconnect between basic terminology and actionable knowledge.
- Bhandari (2025) noted that Nepalese university students show significant gaps in understanding the legal frameworks available for risk mitigation, including the Electronic Transactions Act and the National Cyber Security Policy 2023.
- Ransomware—which encrypts a victim's data and demands payment for decryption—has become increasingly prevalent in higher education settings (Ulven & Wangen, 2021; Bhandari, 2025).
- Bhandari (2025) similarly identified a disconnect between familiarity with hacking terminology (surface-level knowledge) and awareness of legal frameworks and risk mitigation practices (deeper, action-relevant knowledge), consistent with the ISAM prediction that shallow knowledge does not produce protective behaviour.
- Bhandari (2025) argued that legal knowledge constitutes a neglected dimension of risk mitigation: students who understand Nepal's Electronic Transactions Act, the National Cyber Security Policy 2023, and the remedies available to cyber-crime victims are better positioned to seek help, report incidents, and take preventive measures.
- Existing Nepalese studies are either restricted to a single campus (Khatiwada, 2025; Bhandari, 2025) or directed at organisational rather than student populations (Adhikari et al., 2025).
- This operationalisation is consistent with Khatiwada (2025), Bhandari (2025), and Ulven and Wangen (2021).
